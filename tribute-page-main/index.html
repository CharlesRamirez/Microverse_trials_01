Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla

The greatest inventor of all time

Here's a timeline of Nikola Tesla:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Croatia, then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
  • 1862 - He and his family move to the nearby Gospić.
  • 1870 - Moves to Karlovac to attend high school at the Higher Real Gymnasium.
  • 1873 - Graduates from high school, completing a four-year term in three years.
  • 1874 - Evades conscription into the Austro-Hungarian Army in Smiljan by running away southeast of Lika to Tomingaj, near Gračac.
  • 1875 - Enrolles at the Imperial-Royal Technical College in Graz on a Military Frontier scholarship.
  • 1878 - Leaves Graz and severes all relations with his family to hide the fact that he dropped out of college.
  • 1881 - Tesla moves to Budapest, Hungary, to work under Tivadar Puskás at a telegraph company, the Budapest Telephone Exchange.
  • 1884 - Emigrates and begins working almost immediately at the Machine Works on Manhattan's Lower East Side, in US.
  • 1887 - With two other persons, forms the Tesla Electric Company. Also developes an induction motor that runs on alternating current.
  • 1891 - Becomes a naturalized citizen of the United States and he patents his Tesla coil.
  • 1893 - Demonstrates neon light signs and how they can make unique designs and even form words.
  • 1899 - Sets up an experimental station at high altitude in Colorado Springs, where he can safely operate much larger coils than in the cramped confines of his New York laboratory.
  • 1906 - Opens offices at 165 Broadway in Manhattan, trying to raise further funds by developing and marketing his patents.
  • 1913 - Patents a bladeless centripetal flow turbine called Tesla turbine.
  • 1943 - At the age of 86, he dies alone in room 3327 of the Hotel New Yorker.
